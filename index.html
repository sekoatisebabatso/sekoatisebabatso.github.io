Teenage Pregnancy

Teenage pregnancy is pregnancy in females under the age of 20 and it is also known as adolescent pregnancy.

It can occur with sexual intercourse after the start of ovulation which can be before the first menstrual period,but usually occurs after the onset of periods.Teenage pregnancy may be linked to things such as lack of education and information about reproduction,peer pressure and early engagement of sexual activity.These young females have not yet reached adulthood and the cause of teenage pregnancy vary greatly.


In order to prevent teenage pregnancy,teenagers need to have comprehensive understanding of abstinence,contraceptive techniques and consequences.

There are two types of preventing pregnancy.

  1. Natural method
  2. Artificial method

    Ways on how to prevent teenage pregnancy(Natural Method)
  • ASBSTINANCE:It refers to avoiding sexual intercourse altogether.It is 100% safe.
  • SAFE PERIODS:Avoiding intercourse during fertile window or period of menstrual cycle.It is not 100% safe because menstrual cycle are regular and they are affected by emotions,diet and environment.
  • WITHDRAWAL METHOD:A male pulls his penis from vagina just before ejaculation into the female.Ejaculation is the release of sperm through the penis.It is not 100% safe because sperms can come before ejaculation.
    •

    ARTIFICIAL METHOD-include barriers and chemicals and it consists of Contraceptives.Contraceptives refers to different methods used to prevent the process of fertilization. They are also known as Birth control/family planning.

CONDOMS:Are soft rubber bags that are put on the penis and vagina before sexual intercourse.They are barriers because they prevent the seminal fluid of males to be in contact with the walls of vagina.They are not 100% safe since they might burst.No type of condom prevent pregnancy or sexual transmitted diseases(STDs) 100% of the time,but if you and your partner are having having sex,nothing protects against STDs better than the properly used condom.Choose condoms made of of latex which is thought to be most effective in preventing STDs.They can decrease the effectiveness of the condom and increase the chance of pregnancy,as well as sexually transmitted disease.Condoms can be used with lubricants which do not contain oil,such as K-Y Jelly...Condom breakage and slippage can make them less effective.Oil-based lubricant may damage the condom.


PILLS:they are taken by females during specific time.They prevent the release of ovum.They are not 100% safe because the body can not be suppressed by pills.If you start taking combination pills within 5 days after your period stars,you will be protected form pregnancy right away.If you start combination of pills any other time, you need to take the pill for 7 days before you are protected from pregnancy.


INJECTION:This is given to female in three months.It prevents ovum from being released in ovary form being released in ovary. Not 100% save because the body is not suppressed by injections.


SPERMICIDES:These are in the form of cream or jelly.They are applied on the vagina before sexual intercourse so as to kill the sperms.It is not 100% safe because some sperms resists that jelly or cream.
